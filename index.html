Chapter 01 — The Philosophy
"Most inspections take 3 hours. Most buyers spend 3 minutes reading the report. We write reports people actually read."
Read Our Philosophy →

The standard home inspection is a liability document. It's written to protect the inspector, not inform the buyer. Dense boilerplate, vague language, buried findings — by the time you've read page twelve, you've forgotten what was on page three.

We do it differently. Every Sherlock report is organized by severity, written in plain language, and built around what you actually need to know: what's wrong, how wrong it is, and what it will cost you if you ignore it.

The investigation itself is methodical — roof to foundation, systems to structure, attic to crawlspace. We bring thermal imaging to every inspection, not as an upsell, but because heat signatures reveal what the eye cannot. Moisture behind walls. Air leaks in attics. Overloaded circuits hidden inside finished ceilings.

The finding is only half the job. The other half is making sure you understand it before you sign anything.
